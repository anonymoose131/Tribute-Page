Norman "Norm" Macdonald

October 1959 - September 2021

Norm Macdonald, during an interview, on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno on April 21, 2008.

Early Life

  • October 17, 1959 - Norm Macdonald was born in Quebec City, Quebec to Ferne and Percy Lloyd Macdonald. Both of his parents were Anglophone teachers at a military base north of Quebec City.
  • Norm attended Quebec High Schol before moving to Ottawa, Ontario. In Ottawa, Macdonald graduated at the age of 14 from Gloucester High School.
  • At 16, Macdonald enrolled in Carleton University to study mathematics and philosophy. However, he dropped out of Carleton University. He briefly enrolled in Algonquin College's programs for broadcasting-television and journalism, following in his brother Neil Macdonald's footsteps, before dropping out again.
  • 1985 - Norm Macdonald's first performances in comedy consisted of regular appearances in Ottawa on amateur night at Yuk Yuk's. He vowed to never do stand-up comedy again after his first performance because he did not appreciate how well his performance turned out. However, the club owner, Howard Wagman, persuaded him to come back and his confidence grew.
  • 1986 - Macdonald performed at the Just For Laughs Comedy Festival in Montreal. The Montreal Gazette praised him as being, "one of this country's hottest comics."
  • 1988 - Macdonald married Connie Vaillancourt
  • 1990 - Macdonald was a contestant on Star Search and also appeared on Late Night with David Letterman. Letterman was a huge fan of Macdonald and his comedy and he was quoted saying, "If we could have, we would have had Norm on every week."
  • 1992-1993 - Dylan Macdonald, Norm's son, was born this year. Norm Macdonald served as a writer for The Dennis Miller Show and the television sitcom Roseanne before quitting to join the cast of Saturday Night Live (SNL)
  • 1993 - Macdonald joined the cast of SNL where he perfomed many impressions of famous actors, comedians, talk show hosts and many other prevalent figures.
  • 1994 - A new satire segment on SNL was introduced, called Weekend Update with Norm Macdonald as the news anchor. His new segment was met with lots of criticism because of his crude, and brutally honest, sense of humor.
  • 1998 - Macdonald was dismissed from the Weekend Update segment after making a series of jokes about the O.J. Simpson trial, frequently calling him a murderer. The president of NBC's West Coast, Don Ohlmeyer, division turned out to be good friends with O.J. Simpson and wasn't happy about the jokes. Macdonald was dismissed from the SNL shortly after this incident. Macdonald believed that Ohlmeyer's "hatred" toward him influenced the cancellation of promotional appearances for his film, Dirty Work on WNBC's Today in New York, NBC's Late Night with Conan O'Brien, and Access Hollywood.
  • 1998 - In April, Norm and Connie Macdonald seperated and then divorced later this year. Shortly after leaving SNL, Macdonald co-wrote and starred in the "revenge comedy" Dirty Work. The film was directed by Bob Saget, co-starred Artie Lang, and featured Chris Farley, which was Farley's last film before his untimely demise, to which, the film was dedicated. Macdonald also went on to voice the character "Lucky" in the Eddie Murphy adaptation of Dr. Dolittle, Dr. Dolittle 2 (2001), and Dr. Dolittle 3 (2006).
  • 1999 - The Norm Show premiered starring Norm Macdonald, Laurie Metcalf, Artie Lang, and Ian Gomez. Earlier in the year, Macdonald made a cameo appearance in Andy Kaufman's biographical drama called Man on the Moon.
  • 2000 - Macdonald starred alongside Dave Chappelle in the movie Screwed. He also made his first appearance on Family Guy as the voice of Death. Macdonald appeared on the celebrity edition of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire where he won $500,000 for Paul Newman's Hole in the Wall Cherity Camp.
  • 2003 - Macdonald took the role of the title character in a Fox sitcom called A Minute with Stan Hooper.
  • 2005 - Signed a deal with Comedy Central to create the sketch comedy Back to Norm. Later that year, Macdonald voiced a genie named Norm on The Fairly OddParents, a cartoon series on Nickelodeon.
  • 2006 - In September 2006, Macdonald released a sketch comedy album, Ridiculous, with Comedy Central Records. The album featured appearances by Will Ferrell, Artie Lang, Jon Lovitz, Tim Meadows, and Molly Shannon. He created an animated series on the Super Deluxe comedy website entitled The Fake News. Macdonald also filled in for Dennis Miller's weekly "Miller Time" segment on O'Reilly Factor and was a guest-host on Miller's radio show, which he was a brief weekly contributor.
  • 2007 - Guest-starred on an episode of My Name is Earl as "Lil' Chubby", the son of "Chubby".
  • 2008 - Appeared on two episodes of a revived version of the game show Match Show. In August of 2008, Macdonald was a participant in the Comedy Central Roast of Bob Saget where he performed intentionally cheesy and G-rated material, which provided a great contrast to the other roaster's raunchy performances.
  • 2009 - Macdonald reappeared as Burt Reynolds on SNL's segment of Celebrity Jeopardy!. He also appeared on Million Dollar Password.
  • 2010 - Between 2009 and 2010, Macdonald was a frequent guest on The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien. He made frequent appearances on Tom Green's House Tonight, an internet talk show. Macdonald was developing a sports version of The Daily Show for Comedy Central called Sports Show with Norm Macdonald which premiered in April 2011.
  • 2011 - In March, Macdonalds first stand-up special, Me Doing Stand-Up aired on Comedy Central. He also became a commentator and co-host of the seventh season of High Stakes Poker on Game Show Network.
  • 2012 - Became the spokesperson for Safe Auto Insurance Company.
  • 2013 - Macdonald was diagnosed with multiple myeloma. He only disclosed his diagnosis with his family, agent, and producing partner. Macdonald premiered his podcast, Norm Macdonald Live with sidekick Adam Eget. The show streamed live weekly on Video Podcast Network and later posted on YouTube. The podcast received positive feedback from USA Today, Entertainment Weekly and the "America's Comedy" website. Macdonald also made an appearance on Grantland as a contributor in the first two months of the year.
  • 2014 - Campaigned, unsuccessfully, on Twitter to be named the new host of The Late Late Show after Craig Ferguson announced his departure.
  • 2015 - Macdonald became the first stand-up act on the Late Show with David Letterman. During his performance, he broke down into tears after telling Letterman that he truly love him. Macdonald included a joke that Letterman told during an appearance in the 1970's on 90 Minutes Live, a Canadian talk show. Macdonald attended the show as a member of the live studio audience when he was only 13 years-old. Macdonald was also a judge for the ninth season of Last Comic Standing, joining Roseanne Barr and Keenan Ivory Wayans. In August, he succeeded Darrell Hammond as Colornel Sanders in the TV commercials for KFC. He was replaced by Jim Gaffigan by February 2016.
  • 2016 - Randon House imprint published Macdonald's first semi-fictional memoir titled Based on a True Story. The memoir debuted at numer 15 for hardcover non-fiction, and made number 6 for humour, on the New York Times Best Sellers List.
  • 2017 - Macdonald moved his comedy to a more deadpan, reserved style and claimed to have "no opinions". His new minimalist delivery was described as "reducing gesture and verbiage down to an absurd minimum."
  • 2018 - Netflix ordered ten episodes for a new talk show entitled Norm Macdonald Has a Show. The show was hosted by Norm Macdonald and premiered on September 14, 2018. Controversy was sparked when Macdonald criticized aspects of the #MeToo movement where he defended friends Roseann Barr and Louis C.K. Macdonalds appearance on Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon was cancelled after the controversy.
  • 2020 - Macdonald developed treatment-associated myelodysplastic synrome, a cancer that often develops into acute leukemia. Macdonald launched a dating app called Loko which relies on video to make first impressions. He had a standup set prepared for a final Netflix special. Although the special wasn't filmed professionally in front of an audience, Macdonald taped the run through.
  • 2021 - Received his final stem cell transplant and was prescribed dexamethasone which caused him to gain weight. Macdonald suffered from neuropathy after chemotherapy, which led him to give up golf and tennis. He entered the City of Hope National Medical Center in Duarte, California, to receive a round of chemotherapy, where he developed an infection. He remained in the hospital until his death from the acute leukemia on September 14.
  • 2022 - The run through version of the special was released posthumously as Norm Macdonald: Nothing Special on May 30,2022. It was followed by a discussion with comedians Dave Chappelle, Adam Sandler, Molly Shannon, Conan O'Brien, and David Spade. On July 12, 2022, Macdonald was posthumously nominated for three Primetime Emmys for his standup special Norm Macdonald: Nothing Special

"[Norm] elevated live storytelling — distinct in so many ways from “stand-up” — to something beyond an art form. He tricked people. He taunted them, goaded them, left them speechless... But however hard he pushed his audience, he pushed himself harder."

- Kaleb Horton, RollingStone
